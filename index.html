Berke Tan Tabak • Supervisor: Prof. Dr. Seda Dural

Graduation Thesis / Experimental Psychology

Problem

With the rise of AI-generated music, listeners' perception is shaped not only by the sound itself but by their belief about its source.

This study examines the cognitive and neural effects of the discrepancy between the actual source of music and the label presented — a phenomenon we term the "AI-Placebo Effect."

Hypotheses

  • H1 Neural Skepticism: "AI" labels evoke greater Right PFC activation than Human labels.
  • H2 Placebo Bias: "Human" label enhances ratings regardless of actual source.
  • H3 Placebo Mask: Human label suppresses the bias introduced by AI generation.
  • H5/6 Genre Moderation: AI bias differs across Arabesque, Blues, and Electronic.

How We Did It 🔬

We tested 13 participants. They listened to different genres of music.

We told them the music was either made by a "Human" or an "AI". While they listened, we recorded their brain activity using a safe headband (fNIRS) that uses light to see which parts of the brain are working hard!

Design

2×2×3 Within-Subjects Design Matrix
N = 13 Participants
12 Trials / Subject
Label: AI vs Human Source: AI vs Human Genre: 3 Types

Counterbalanced presentation. Label assigned orthogonally to actual source.

"AI" LABEL ↑ Right PFC Peak HbO by +54%
Linear Mixed-Effects (LME): β1 = 0.058, SE = 0.035, p = .047
Right PFC Peak HbO (µM): AI [0.168], Human [0.109]
Right Prefrontal HbO signals suggest differential cognitive engagement and skepticism.
Brain scans showed different activity when people thought an AI made the music.

THE LABEL ALTERS COGNITIVE LOAD

"AI" labeling significantly increased Right Prefrontal Cortex activation independent of the actual music source.

/ Key Takeaways

  • 🏷️ The Label Overrides the Sound:
    Even when the music is identical, knowing it's "AI" changes how you listen to it.
  • 🧐 Built-in Skepticism:
    The "AI" label activates the brain area associated with critical evaluation and distrust.
  • 🎭 The Placebo Mask:
    Slapping a "Human" label on an AI song can actually make people like it more.

Discussion

Our data suggests that human perception of art is heavily mediated by the mental models we hold about the creator. The AI label acts as a 'distrust cue', increasing evaluative effort.
